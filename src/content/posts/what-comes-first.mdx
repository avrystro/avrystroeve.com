I was making $4,000 a month building a business. It wasn't just remote sales anymore, it was the whole thing: running ads, making cold calls, designing campaigns, doing operations. Traveling with my friend who started the company.

I'd also just finished Landmark Forum and worked through years of stuff I'd been carrying since my dad passed. Seemed like a lot was going right..

Yet there was still a hole eating itself away inside of me.

In Valencia, Spain I'd take walks and just think 'for what purpose was I doing it all for?' For what purpose was I building this business, or traveling, or even existing and living? It wasn't depression or loneliness - it was the absence of something I couldn't name. Something genuinely missing..

Then I got summoned for Jury Duty. Flew back from Spain, where I'd been living. While I was home I was put onto the Self-Realization Fellowship lessons from Paramahansa Yogananda - the author who also wrote Autobiography of a Yogi, which for those of you who don't know, is the one book Steve Jobs recommended to everyone at his memorial.

I just looked up the day I bought the lessons: October 17th was the day I started..

## What's your take on God?

Genuinely, send me a message on this writing somewhere it should be here somewhere and tell me: What's your take on God?

I'll share what mine used to be, and what it is now:

I used to be one of those "spiritual people" trying to find presence. I wanted to always be "present in the moment", and thought that's what all these enlightened people had found. But even after years of that being my objective, I still found myself never quite able to keep it. It wasn't easy and effortless to keep at all times.

And I figured out why recently, because things changed. I basically completely stopped trying to be present. And in its place began spending a small portion of my mornings and nights in stillness, placing all concentration on the infinite indweller within everything you see around you right now that has been called God, Allah, Truth, the One, Source, among many others.

Through that concentration, I realized that feeling of me chasing presence was actually me chasing God. It was just a partially accurate, incomplete yearning for the greater whole which is God. Presence is a ray, God is the sun. Seek the sun.

Now:

God is a FEELING. It's sitting in silence feeling the stillness and hearing your own heartbeat drumming in your body. Pulsing as blood moves all around and within you. The feeling of that force that keeps all life around you going, that is also within you.

## What's the most scientific way to reach God?

That practice of meditation at its core, means one thing: concentration on God.

Here's what it looks like for me. I sit down. Chair, good posture. At home or outside in nature. Eyes up, focused on the point between my eyebrows, the third eye. That's the doorway.

First, breathing exercises. Paramahansa Yogananda teaches these in the lessons. They're designed to get rid of the carbon dioxide in your body so you can reach stillness. Because your body is restless. Three sources of noise: your legs, your breath, your thoughts. You quiet all three until you don't even notice your own breathing.

Then I repeat one word. God. Over and over. That word becomes the only thing in my mind.

And you feel it. I'm not going to oversell what happens next because you have to experience it yourself. But I'll say this: the better you get at this concentration, the more it helps every single aspect of your life.

This isn't something I do once a morning and check a box. It's throughout the day. Walking, working, eating. The more often you bring your mind back to God, the more constant the connection becomes. It goes from a practice to a presence.

That hole I was talking about? It was this. It was always this.

## The Order

Once God was first, everything else fell into place. Five priorities, ranked:

**1. God.** Everything I just said. The foundation. Everything else falls apart without it.

**2. Health.** You can't serve from a broken body. Stretching, clean food, movement. Treat the body like it's sacred, because it is.

**3. Intellectual Community.** Not networking. People who expand your mind and pull more truth out of you. The people around you determine the quality of your thinking.

**4. Prosperity for Self and Others.** After God, health, and community. Not before. Revenue is service. Helping someone help themselves. The money follows the first three.

**5. Social Status and Popularity.** Last. On purpose. Popularity that comes from living the first four is earned. Popularity chased first is hollow.

Paramahansa Yogananda talks about the simplicity of living. That's what this is. When you know the order, you stop agonizing over every decision. You just check it against the list.

Life is simple.
